Our vision

is to trace the collective behavior of interacting quantum degrees of freedom to an underlying principle of motion. At present, we do this by starting from a principle, such as:

  • the trapping of electrons on an atom in a Mott insulating phase;
  • the fluctuations of spin moments within the high dimensional space available to them in a frustrated antiferromagnets;
  • the restriction of fermions to live near their Fermi surface.
We then seek collective behavior arising in such situations. In the future, we may also be interested in starting from the collective behavior, such as:
  • topological protected/constrained motion
  • gauge dynamics in condensed matter
  • metal-insulator transitions
and seeking the underlying principle.
